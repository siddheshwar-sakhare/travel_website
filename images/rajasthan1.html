Some of the Must Visit Attractions in Rajasthan Holiday Packages

Amber Fort, Jaipur-Amber Fort, also known as Amer Fort, is one of the must-see places in Jaipur. This massive fort, built in the 16th century, may look old from the outside, but it is miraculously beautiful on the inside.

Hawa Mahal, Jaipur-This pink and red palace is a beautiful architectural tourist attraction in Jaipur. Due to its more than 900 windows on the outside wall of the building, it is renowned as Hawa Mahal, the "Palace of the Winds."

Jantar Mantar, Jaipur-Jantar Mantar is an astronomical site built in the 18th century for observing astronomical bodies with the naked eye. Jantar Mantar has the world's largest stone sundial.

City Palace of Jaipur-The City Palace is a complex comprising Mubarak Mahal and the Maharani Palace. It is actually the residence of the royal family and has a collection of royal items in the museum named after the founder of Jaipur, Maharaja Sawai Man Singh II.

Ranthambore National Park, Swai Madhopur-Just 150 kilometres away from Jaipur, this national park is located in the Aravalli and Vindhya Mountain ranges. This adventurous site may allow you to encounter tigers in the wild.

Lake Pichola, Udaipur-Lake Pichola is one of the most beautiful lakes in Udaipur. The serenity you can feel when boating in the middle of the lake surrounded by beautiful palaces and hills all around is unparalleled.

Chittorgarh Fort-This one of the largest forts in India will make you feel the bravery of the Rajputs. It is a complex of many palaces and temples within the fort.

TIMING

The Best Time to Visit Rajasthan for a Memorable Vacation

Rajasthan boasts a unique climate distinct from other northern states in India, owing to its geographical location. Situated between the Aravalli Mountain ranges and the Thar Desert, the state enjoys a mild climate. Rajasthan experiences three primary seasons: winter, monsoon, and summer. The ideal time to visit depends on your planned activities, but it is advisable to avoid the summer season. The most favorable months to explore Rajasthan are from October to March. The winter season ensures a comfortable visit for all activities, although night time temperatures can significantly drop, so packing extra blankets is recommended.

Summer in Rajasthan
Temp Range - 24C to 48C April to June

With the onset of April, summer spreads its scorching embrace over Rajasthan, persisting until June. During this time, temperatures soar between 24C and 48C, making it quite uncomfortable for tourists. The intense heat makes this season unfavorable for travel and exploration.

Monsoon in Rajasthan
Temp Range - 21C to 35C July to September

As July arrives, Rajasthan welcomes the monsoon season, which continues until September. The temperature ranges from 21C to 35C, creating a pleasant atmosphere. Monsoon clouds gather and thunderclaps pave the way for heavy rainfall, rejuvenating the arid landscapes of the region. This season is also favorable for travel, as the refreshing showers add a touch of enchantment to Rajasthan's vibrant cultural and natural treasures.

Winter in Rajasthan
Temp Range - 13C to 30C October to March

As October arrives, Rajasthan transitions into the delightful embrace of winter, which extends until March. During this season, temperatures range from 13C to 30C, creating a pleasant and comfortable environment. The weather during winter is highly favorable for travel, thanks to the pleasant daytime temperatures. Exploring the enchanting attractions of Rajasthan becomes even more enjoyable as you embrace the crisp air and revel in the cultural and historical wonders of the region.

HOW TO REACH

How to Reach Rajasthan?

By Air

Rajasthan has three major airports: Sanganer International Airport in Jaipur, Debok airport in Udaipur, and Jodhpur airport. Jaipur handles both domestic and international flights, while Debok and Jodhpur only handle domestic flights. Air is the most convenient way to reach Rajasthan. You can choose your destination based on your itinerary.

By Train

Railways are a convenient and economical way to travel to and within Rajasthan. The major railway stations in Rajasthan, such as Jaipur, Ajmer, Udaipur, and Kota, connect to all major cities in India, such as Delhi, Kolkata, Mumbai, Hyderabad, and Chennai. You can choose to commute to different locations from there, such as Bharatpur, Bikaner, Alwar, Jodhpur, Jaisalmer, and others, by train.

By Road

Rajasthan is well connected by numerous national and state highways. The NH8, which connects Delhi to Mumbai, runs along major cities in Rajasthan, such as Jaipur, Ajmer, Udaipur, and Chittorgarh. The state highway, which expands to a length of more than 10,000 kilometers, will connect you to every corner of the state. You can choose to drive or take the bus, depending on your convenience.

  • Jaipur: Jaipur, the capital city of Rajasthan, has a rich heritage worth boasting about. This can be seen in the magnificent monuments like Hawa Mahal, City Palace, and Amer Fort.

More Information on Rajasthan Tourism

Rajasthan, a North Indian state renowned for its heritage, is among the most visited places by travel enthusiasts from around the globe. Rajasthan is also known as the Land of Maharajas. The capital, Jaipur, is part of the Golden Triangle, which attracts Indian and international tourists alike. The state is home to numerous historical forts and palaces, known for their unique and intriguing facts. Alongside these architectural wonders, Rajasthan boasts distinctive temples deeply rooted in its culture and history. Visitors are also captivated by the state's vibrant fairs, festivals, and functions, as well as the exploration of majestic palaces once inhabited by its rulers. In addition to its rich cultural heritage, Rajasthan's wildlife experiences draw people in. With over 30 national parks and wildlife sanctuaries, the state provides a habitat for diverse wildlife, including the majestic Indian tigers

Exclusive Highlights of Rajasthan Tourism Packages

When it comes to tourism, the incredible Rajasthan has many attractions in its bucket that draw millions of travelers from across the globe every year. Here are the major highlights of Rajasthan tourism holidays.

Exploring Majestic Historical Marvels

The history of Rajasthan and the Rajputs has left a majestic architectural heritage that will allow you to easily feel the grandeur of the rulers of those days. Amer Fort (Jaipur), Chittor Fort (Chittorgarh), and Ranthambore Fort (Sawai Madhopur) are some of the best places to visit in Rajasthan.

Vibrant Festivals & Fairs

Rajasthan has its own unique list of festivals and fairs that are organized throughout the year by the Tourism Department. As these are major tourist attractions, you should plan accordingly to ensure that you can visit one of the festivals during your tour.

Desert Adventures

The Thar Desert terrain of Rajasthan is a whole different experience for most tourists. The desert visit comes with a lot of activities that cannot even be completed in a single day visit. Along with desert camping, watching sunrise and sunset, and stargazing at night, you can also participate in activities such as dune bashing, parasailing, camel rides, and traditional performances by local music artists.

Wildlife Adventures

Rajasthan is home to many wild animals living in the vast number of sanctuaries across the state. This vast ecosystem has many mammals that can be encountered in the sanctuaries such as Ranthambore National Park and Sariska National Park.

Luxury Heritage Hotels & Palaces

Rajasthan has always been a center of luxury, whether it is jewels, residences, or events. Heritage hotels built in the magnificent historical palaces make your dining experience special and luxurious.

Spiritual Retreats

People looking for calm and serene places to explore have a lot of options in Rajasthan. Spiritual and religious retreats are as royal and luxurious as the palaces here. Birla Mandir, Brahma Temple, and Ajmer Sharif are some of the most visited spiritual places in Rajasthan.

Shopping and Handicrafts

Art and color flow through the streets of Rajasthan. You are sure to find something in the bazaars that will make you remember your trip to Rajasthan for a lifetime. Markets such as Bada Bazaar, Bapu Bazaar, and Johari Bazaar are no less than a paradise for shopaholics.

Top Things to Do on a Rajasthan Holiday

Whether you're planning for a week or just a couple of days, you wouldn't want to miss the exciting array of activities that Rajasthan has to offer. From breathtaking experiences to adorable encounters, here are some must-include activities for your trip to Rajasthan.

  • Trekking - Mount Abu and Udaipur have good trekking trails.
  • Elephant Rides - Elephant rides are an enjoyable activity that can be done near Amer Fort.

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride - Seeing the architectural beauty from the air can be a lifetime memory for you. Hot air balloon rides can be found in Jaipur.
  • Camel Safari - An exclusive adventure in the Thar Desert filled with splendid views of dunes will give you the whole desert experience.
  • Desert Camping- Camping under the clear night sky of the desert is a peaceful experience. You can enjoy the local music, puppet show, and stargazing.
  • Wildlife Jungle Safari - The wildlife of Rajasthan attracts tourists with as much enthusiasm as its forts and palaces. Ranthambore, Sariska, and Jhalana are some of the popular national parks in Rajasthan for jungle safaris.
The Perfect Souvenirs to Buy from Rajasthan: A Guide to Shopping in the State

Rajasthan has countless souvenirs for you to take home as a memory of your trip to Rajasthan.

Jewels - Johri Bazaar (Jaipur)

Rajasthanis carve some of the uniquely best gemstones and jewels that you will love to buy for your loved one.

Handicrafts - Nehru Market (Jaipur)

The handicraft section of Rajasthan offers a wide variety of items, including textiles, bangles, pottery, toys, home decoratives, and other goods. These items are small and cheap, but they still depict the attractive craftsmanship of this Indian state.

Leather Products - Mahatma Gandhi Road (Jaipur)

Rajasthan produces leather products, including juttis (shoes) and carry bags made from camel leather. These are some of the must-buy items from the Rajasthani market.

Clothes and Fashion - Nehru Bazaar (Jaipur)

Rajasthan has a totally different kind of clothing fashion. The designs are colorful and embroidered with hand-printed patterns, making them adorable.

Wooden and Stone Carved Articles - Kishanpole and Chandpole Bazaars (Jaipur)

Just like the fine carved palaces, temples, and forts of historic Rajasthan, people here are still masters of carved articles. You may find fine showpieces of wood or stone throughout Rajasthan.

Puppets and Ttoys (Jaipur, Udaipur, Bikaner, Ajmer)

Stuffed toys and puppets known as kathputli are easily available throughout Rajasthan's markets. They depict the common people of Rajasthan, and are thus made only in traditional dresses of Rajasthani people. These colorful toys can be used as home decor.
